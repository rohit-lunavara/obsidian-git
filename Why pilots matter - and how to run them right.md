# References

> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> Pilots are not a box-ticking exercise, it is the sales process
> 	Builds momentum, validates value, sets up for a fast close
> 	
> Goals:
> 	1. Integrations and data pipes - APIs connected, data flowing and workflows setup
> 	2. Early results that clarify your value - the customer should feel the benefit quickly
> 	3. Volume assessment - prevents surprises later and ensures both have confidence in the annual contract structure
> 
> Define clear milestones, deliverables and timelines - include a clear owner for the pilot inside the customer org, plus someone on your side
> 
> Pilots stall when:
> 	1. Value prop isnt immediately clear
> 	2. No owner for the pilot/no internal resources
> 	3. Vague/undefined milestones
> 	4. Work feels optional since the pilot is free
> 
> Protect momentum at all costs - <3 months, ideally 1 month only
> 
> Pilots shouldnt be free since humans dont value what they dont pay for
> 	Should be a token amount - it signals seriousness
> Paid pilots validate willingness to pay, check if customer fits ICP, avoids wasting time on tire kickers
> 
> Pricing formula:
> 	1. Determine duration - estimate time for integrations, deliver results early, assess usage - same as goals
> 	2. Discounted monthly price - covers costs, roughly in line with a prorated portion of anticipated annual contract, 20-50% discount to lower friction
> 	3. Use pricing to create urgency - charge monthly during the pilot, but include a clause that credits the pilot cost towards the annual contract if the customer moves forward within a defined period
