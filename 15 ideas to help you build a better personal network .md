> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> 1. Stop asking to “pick people’s brains” - be specific about what you are looking for and why you are reaching out
> 2. Make it easy for people to help you
> 3. Send thank you emails - Simple, but very effective; make sure to mention a specific detail from the conversation
> 4. Don't talk poorly about other people - if you're gossiping, people will expect you to gossip about them and inherently trust you less
> 5. Aim to actually be helpful
> 6. Practice active listening - pay close attention to what people say and clarify as the conversation evolves
> 7. Beware of interrupting
> 8. Remember small details - consider writing them down or associating them with people
> 9. Ask unique questions - ask people what they think about particular topics; ask why a few times; be uniquely specific
> 10. Hold judgements - don't bucket people into one of a few categories
> 11. Keep people top of mind - if you find something that makes you think of someone, send it to them
> 12. Beware of overpromising and underdelivering - err on the side of underpromising and overdelivering
> 13. Be consistent
> 14. Find optimism in other people's work
> 15. Remember where you started
