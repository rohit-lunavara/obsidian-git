> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> Reflection - take time to introspect and figure out why it is that you do not love you job
> 	It takes some critical thinking and internal self-awareness
> 	It often takes creating distance for yourself so you can assess your situation more directly, like a third-party would without getting too emotional and defensive and blind
> 
> Write about this: "What is going on at work?" for 15 minutes
> 	Write 1500-3000 words
> 
> Then, do this:
> 	Open up your calendar. Go back a week. What meetings gave you energy? What meetings took away energy from you? Why? Write about this.
> 	Open Slack. Which people give you energy? What people take away energy from you? Why? Write about this.
> 	Open docs or work you’ve done. Which activities gave you energy? Which took away from you? Why? Write about this.
> 
> What you’re trying to do is have a more refined point of view on what’s going on
> 
> Give yourself language to use to describe your feelings
> It’ll help you make wiser decisions that you won’t come to regret years later
> 
> You can easily go your whole life bouncing between companies constantly complaining about work
> 
> You not loving your job is not an immediate reason to jump ship and join the next one
> 
> You can explore without needing to make a full-on change
> 	Try to make changes at your current job
> 	Try to make the situation work
> 	See if the things that are important to you to be resolved can be resolved
>
