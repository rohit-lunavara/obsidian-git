# References

> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> Prevent both aversion and overreliance
> 
> Trust comes from calibrating reliance - by making decisions easier to verify and matching the user's domain expertise
> 
> Aversion - 
> 	1. Opacity
> 	2. Negative past exp
> 	3. No/low sense of control
> 	4. No ability to override
> 
> Overreliance - 
> 	1. Unverifiable explanations
> 	2. Persuasion instead of insight
> 	3. Satisfaction driven UX where positive feedback is favored over critical evaluation
> 	4. Cognitive offloading
> 
> Design Patterns for Building Trust
> ![[Pasted image 20260326023105.png]]
> 
> 5 foundations of user trust - 
> 	1. Reliability - product behaves as expected
> 	2. Technical competence - perceived intelligence, capability of the product
> 	3. Understandability - product must articulate how a decision came along
> 	4. Faith and care - belief that the product wants a positive outcome for you
> 	5. Personal attachment - rapport, connection with the product
> 	   
> Design patterns - 
> 	1. Dont 'ask me anything' - push for scoping and constraints
> 	2. Slow down prompting - request specific details (similar to how I ask the agent to clarify ambiguities when building a plan)
> 	3. Present many viewpoints - explain that experts disagree as well
> 	4. Highlight what is AI and what isn't
> 	5. Allow tweak/override of AI suggestions
> 	6. Adapt AI's tone depending on the severity of the user's task
