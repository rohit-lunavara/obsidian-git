# References

> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> Hackathons are an innovation engine, but only if separate from work
> 	You should work on totally new things
> 	You should focus 100% on the hackathon
> 	Give permission and cover for your team to focus on the hackathon and ignore day-to-day work
> 
> Hackathons are for everyone
> 	Getting unfamiliar parts of the org to work together breaks down silos between technical and non-technical teammates
> 
> Demos aren't optional
> 	You want to have something worth showing off, so initially ambitious plans quickly become more realistic to ship something real
> 	Judging and prizes are optional and arguably undesirable
> 	Keep track of what people demo, it's easy for these valuable ideas to get lost
> 
> The best hackathon projects ship
> 	Deadend projects are a part of the messy process of innovation, but everyone starts knowing that great projects can end up in production
> 	Encourage people to "be the driver" to take their project as far as they can and give them some slack after the hackathon for them to do it
> 
> Make hackathons a tradition
> 	People are more likely to share ideas throughout the year when they have a space for it and know they might actually get worked on
> 	Many hackathon ideas are just inspo for features that should be built as part of a team's day to day work; a roadmap often misses the ambitious ideas hackathons encourage
> 	Its a perk and cultural cornerstone since it shows that the company values autonomy
