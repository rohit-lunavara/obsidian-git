> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> It’s common to see someone ask a pretty generic high-level question, and then be surprised when a) people aren’t that interested in answering and b) the answers they do receive aren’t exactly that useful
> 
> chances are one of two things may be happening:
> 1. You’re being indirect
> 2. You still need to process what you actually want
> 
> Practical method for getting better advice and feedback from others
> 1. Understand for yourself why you’re asking for help
> 	1. Do the work internally to figure out what you actually want
> 	2. You can have a fuzzy desire to connect with others
> 2. Look for the right person
> 3. Do your research - See if you can get what you want without needing to actually talk to the person
> 4. Make your asks extremely clear
> 	1. People want to help, but its hard to respond to something very general
> 5. Share your motivation
> 	1. Don't just explain what you want, explain the why
> 6. Set the other person up for success
> 	1. Share relevant context and info they need to be able to make a useful contribution without lots of back and forth
> 	2. Don't put them in an uncomfortable position
> 7. Reduce the cost needed for someone else to help you
> 	1. If you want them to email someone on your behalf, send them a forward-able email
