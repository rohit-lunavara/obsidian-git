> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> 1. Gather the right context
> Good context points teams in the right direction; great context can change a company's entire trajectory
> Types of context:
> 	1. Competitor landscape
> 	2. User research
> 	3. Industry news
> 	4. Customer feedback
> It is not always obvious what info is useful at any given point in time
> Track what types of context led to measurable success through accountability loops
> For engineers:
> 	Don't wait around for someone else to give you the right context for your product
> 	E.g. Talking to users provides clearer insight than reading secondhand notes from your PM
> 	Sometimes onlt the person with the full context of a problem can ask the right questions

> 2. Track success with feedback loops
> ![[Pasted image 20260521231714.png]]
> Feedback loops like these create accountability and, in turn, give developers more autonomy
> For engineers:
> 	Turn every sprint into a mini growth review by setting aside time to define and evaluate success in each session
> 	1. Nailed it: We hit the goal spectacularly. High fives all round.
> 	2. Scraped it: We almost hit the goal, but we need to do a bit more to tidy up.
> 	3. Failed it: We were nowhere near hitting the goal, but we learned some valuable lessons. Back to the drawing board for this one.

> 3. Communicate towards action
> Making communication actionable reduces friction by surfacing important info from the start:
> 	1. Stating the impact
> 	2. Being specific
> 	3. Sharing relevant context
> 	4. Making trade-offs visible
> 	5. Having an opinion
> For engineers:
> 	Default to shipping when you can
> 	PRs are the gold-standard of communication
> 	When the next step is unclear and you need to bring it up for discussions, work backwards and figure out the context, success criteria that will make the info as actionable as possible
