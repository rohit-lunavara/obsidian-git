> [!QUOTE] Original Capture
> 1. You do have experience - Marketing is simply closing the gap between what you're building and the people who'd care about it
> 	1. Figure out what your audience finds interesting and talk about it
> 2. Depth-first > breadth-first - Going niche forces you to develop something unique in a way that others can't copy
> 3. Match your channels to your sales motion - If unsure about PLG vs SLG, define ICP first
> 	1. If you are seeing early signs of PLG working, keep going
> 	2. If you are sales-led, early wins are going to come from more targeted approaches
> 	3. Don't start with SEO/AEO, it takes a very long time and lots of discipline
> 4. Think in terms of experiments - Before starting anything new, write down your prediction for what "worked" would look like; a simple yes/no question
> 	1. Don't choose a metric target at the early stage
> 	2. It has to be unambiguous whether you succeeded or failed
> 	3. 6 weeks is enough for an experiment
> 5. Attribution is a lie
> 	1. Don't try to build/buy a fancy multi-touch attribution software
> 	2. Add a "where did you hear about us?" text box in your signup flow - single highest-signal piece of marketing data
> 	3. Trust attribution when the signal is overwhelming, ignore when ambiguous
> 	4. Text box > Attribution data
> 6. Don't outsource to an agency
> 	1. An agency can't build your positioning, understand your users, or decide what "good" looks like for your brand - don't delegate it until you've done it yourself
> 	2. When you learn what types of marketing works well for your target audience, it will also improve product decisions
> 	3. Only use agencies for things you genuinely can't do internally
> 	4. Never use agencies to replace thinking you haven't done yet
> 7. When to make the first hire
> 	1. You need: users who actually use the product, users who enthusiastically recommend it to other people, users who pay for it
> 	2. Hire a generalist first - they should be able to write, think in funnels, talk to users and figure things out without a playbook
> 	3. They should be pretty experienced since you won't have a long term strategy to present to them
